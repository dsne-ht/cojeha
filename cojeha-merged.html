Reason to Keep Going
2017

Built on
big belief.

COJEHA was founded in 2017 by Nancy Michel with a simple conviction: that Haiti's youth deserved more than what the system was giving them. We started with school fees and the belief that keeping a child in a classroom could change the trajectory of a life.

When gang violence forced us out of our Port-au-Prince headquarters in 2023, we didn't close down. We stripped back to what mattered most — and kept paying school fees. Through everything, the children stayed enrolled. In 2024–2025, our school subvention program grew by more than 80%.

Now we are replanting in the Nord-Est, on land we own, with a plan that is bigger than anything we've done before — because the need has only grown.

What we do.

Our programs are built around one question: what does a child in the Nord-Est actually need to stay in school, stay healthy, and have a future? The answers have shaped everything we do.

01

School Subvention

We cover school fees for students across the Nord-Est, ensuring financial hardship is never a reason to leave the classroom. This is our most consistent, most direct intervention — and the one we have maintained through every disruption. The program grew by over 80% in 2024–2025. Our goal for 2026 is to more than double that.
Active+
02

Mobile Health Clinics

Bringing free medical consultations and medications directly to communities that cannot access formal health infrastructure — including isolated rural areas and detention facilities. Built on partnerships with existing health actors in the Nord-Est department. This is where we are investing next.
In Planning+
03

Youth Recognition Ceremonies

Gatherings that remind students they are seen, they matter, and that their progress deserves to be celebrated. Community presence as a form of investment. We bring students, parents, school directors, and local partners together in one room.
Active+
04

Agricultural Entrepreneurship Program

Our foundational farm management and entrepreneurship program reached 14 communes at its peak. Temporarily paused due to land loss and funding constraints. Revival is part of the long-term plan — the knowledge, the relationships, and the land are still there.
Paused+
05

Youth Sustainability Contest

Annual competition where students across the Nord-Est write and present their vision for sustainable development in their communities. Real judges, real stakes, real futures. First held across 14 communes. Returning in 2026.
Returning 2026+
06

Community & Institutional Partnerships

We are building sustained relationships with local schools, health actors, and departmental institutions — the kind of partnerships that outlast any single organization or funding cycle. Current partners include schools across the Nord-Est and regional health infrastructure.
Active+
